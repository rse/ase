
Agentic Software Engineering
============================

-   Definition: **Software**:<br/>
    The collection of instructions and data that tell a computer how to
    work.

-   Definition: **Engineering**:<br/>
    The systematic application of scientific and technological
    knowledge, principles, approaches, practices, methods, and
    experiences, to design, build, operate, and maintain complex
    production-grade solutions through a traceable process.

-   Definition: **Software Engineering**:<br/>
    The systematic application of **Engineering** knowledge, principles,
    approaches, practices, methods, and experiences to the development
    of **Software**.

-   Definition: **Software Agent**:<br/>
    **Software** that acts autonomously on behalf of a user or another
    software to achieve delegated goals. Exhibits autonomy, reactivity
    (responds to environment), proactivity (goal-directed initiative),
    and social ability (interacts with other agents/users). Perceives
    its environment via sensors/inputs (APIs, files, messages,
    events) and acts via actors/outputs (commands, calls, messages).
    Distinguished from a plain program/function by autonomy and
    persistence &mdash; it runs continuously, makes decisions, and pursues
    goals without step-by-step instruction.

-   Definition: **Artificial Intelligence (AI)**:<br/>
    The field of Computer Science focused on building systems that
    perform tasks normally requiring human intelligence: perception,
    reasoning, learning, planning, decision-making, and language
    understanding. Spans Symbolic AI (rules, logic, search), Machine
    Learning (statistical pattern learning from data), Deep Learning
    (neural networks), and Generative AI (LLMs, diffusion models).
    Operates along a spectrum from narrow AI (task-specific, e.g. image
    classifiers) to hypothetical AGI (general, human-level capability).

-   Definition: **AI Agent**:<br/>
    **Software Agent**, driven by an LLM (or similar **AI**
    model) as the reasoning core, wrapped in a control loop (e.g.
    Plan-Act-Observe-Reflect or Plan-and-Execute).

-   Definition: **Agentic AI**:<br/>
    A class of **AI** systems that act as **Software Agents**
    &mdash; they perceive, plan, decide, act, and adapt to achieve
    high-level goals, not just answer single prompts. Distinct from
    passive/generative AI by the fact that Agentic AI takes actions in
    the world (calls APIs, runs code, edits files, browses, orchestrates
    sub-agents).

-   Definition: **Agentic Software Engineering**:<br/>
    Software Engineering supported by **AI Agents** to perform
    tasks across the software development lifecycle. Goes beyond AI
    autocomplete/copilots toward multi-step operations. Engineers shift
    toward goal specification, oversight, review, and governance, with
    human-in-the-loop gates.
